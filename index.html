The agentic workflow I built for Resilient

Quantifying climate risks on industrial sites with 100+ specialised LLM agents.

Context

I co-founded Resilient, a SaaS platform helping industrial companies assess and quantify climate-related risks on their facilities. Our clients included major groups such as Thales, Safran, FORVIA, Clayens, and Hutchinson.

Industrial sites are increasingly exposed to climate hazards like floods, heatwaves, hail, and storms. Risk managers needed a scalable way to identify vulnerabilities, estimate potential production shutdown costs, and prioritise adaptation investments.

What I built

I designed and implemented an agentic GenAI workflow combining RAG, variable extraction, and quantification models to evaluate the impact of climate hazards at scale. The system is powered by 100+ specialised LLM agents.

  • Processing hundreds of pages of insurance reports and climate audits
  • Extracting 100+ variables required for our simulation models from raw documents
  • Feeding a quantitative model estimating physical damages and business interruption

Tech stack

  • Python
  • Streamlit
  • Docker
  • OpenAI / Mistral / Anthropic
  • LangChain

Impact

We ran 9 POCs with major industrial groups, identifying critical risks such as potential production shutdowns of more than one month—representing tens of millions of euros in avoided losses per site. The workflow reduced analysis time and cost for risk managers by a factor of five, enabling site-level adaptation planning at scale.
